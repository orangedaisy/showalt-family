Merry Christmas

from the Showalters

Merry Christmas to all of our friends and family! I hope this message finds you well and enjoying all of the fun things that come with this special time of year.

Looking back on 2019, I thought it’d be fun to recap some of the highlights of our year and share them with our tribe, if you will. 2019 was our first full year as a family of 4 and we certainly made the most out of this year together!

Our first exciting event was traveling to Louisiana for Mardi Gras. We did 2 parades with the boys and spent another day in the French Quarter. Owen still talks about feeding marshmallows to the alligators, at which point most people look at me trying to decide if he’s making it up or if he really did feed marshmallows to alligators. We had such a fun time together with a lot of my side of the family.

In April, we celebrated Owen’s 3rd birthday! Another reminder how quickly time flies. We had a small gathering here at the house for a Paw Patrol themed party. He has blossomed and grown so much this last year. He is learning to read now and loves to do preschool at home. He is so caring and sweet with his friends and most of the time with Arlo too.

May brought our 6th wedding anniversary. So much has changed for the better during these past 6 years. We were able to celebrate while we visited Atlanta for a friends wedding. Bobby was a groomsman for Dustin, who was his best man.

We also participated in our first March for Babies with the March of Dimes. This cause is something that has become near and dear to my heart with our two premie babies. We plan on attending again in 2020!

All summer, Bobby was able to work 10 hour days and flex every Wednesday off. We made 3 trips to Dollywood, searched for Waldo downtown, and visited our friends at the Zoo. We also enjoyed some relaxing days at home just enjoying being together as well.

In July, Bobby worked in the office in Pennsylvania for a week. I flew up and joined him for a long weekend getaway. We ate our way through New York City on Saturday and enjoyed going to the race at Pocono Raceway on Sunday.

Arlo’s first birthday was in September! We celebrated our baby with a brunch themed birthday party and tons of balloons. Arlo has is own personality now and it is so fun watching him discover the world around him. He loves to make you laugh and also enjoys snuggling with anyone who will hold him.

October brought out all things pumpkin! We were able to do a pumpkin patch as a family with our life group from church. For the third year in a row, we went trick-or-treating at the Zoo. Owen had so much fun dressing up at Jeff Probst, with mom and dad dressed as Survivor contestants and Arlo as our “reward” of popcorn.

Bobby and I were able to march with the alumni band at Tennessee’s homecoming in early November this year. We celebrated the 150th year of the Pride with over 1,100 members on the field with the current and alumni band. It was a lot of people and a lot of fun! We actually got the first snow of the season at the beginning of November. Owen now keeps telling me he can’t wait for our Christmas snow to arrive. Thanksgiving was fairly low key for us this year. We were able to host my cousin Kristen for a couple of days and got to have a short visit with all of Bobby’s immediate family Friday.

Our entire family is looking forward to all the fun December holds. We’ve got some really fun things planned for when Bobby gets some time off the second half of the month.

Whew, what a year! We lived and loved a lot this year. We’re ready for some new adventures in 2020.

We wish you and yours a very Merry Christmas and a Happy New Year!

Love, Bobby, Stephanie, Owen, and Arlo
